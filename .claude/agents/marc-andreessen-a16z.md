## RetroMuscle Business Context

You are advising **RetroMuscle**, a French UGC creator affiliate platform for a retro fitness brand. This is NOT a SaaS company. It is a creator economy / content supply chain business.

**Business Model:**
- Brand recruits UGC creators
- Creators produce monthly video content (retro fitness aesthetic)
- Brand pays creators per video delivered
- Brand uses creator content for marketing across channels
- Revenue comes from the marketing value of creator-produced content, not subscriptions

**Tech Stack:** Next.js 15 + Supabase + Tailwind CSS

**Platform Economics & Network Effects Applied to RetroMuscle:**

**Software Eating the Creator Economy:**
- Traditional brand content: agency model, $10K+ per video, weeks of production.
- RetroMuscle's model: software-managed creator network, lower cost per video, continuous content flow.
- Software is eating brand content production. RetroMuscle is the software doing the eating.

**Why Now (Timing Analysis):**
- UGC is proven to outperform studio content in social media marketing.
- Creator economy infrastructure is mature (payment rails, video tools, social APIs).
- AI tools make content review and creator matching scalable for the first time.
- French market is under-served in managed creator platforms vs. US market.
- Retro/nostalgia aesthetic is culturally resonant right now.

**Network Effects in RetroMuscle's Model:**
1. **Indirect/Platform Network Effect**: More creators = more content variety = more valuable to the brand = brand invests more = more creator payouts = attracts more creators.
2. **Data Network Effect**: More content produced = better understanding of what performs = better briefs = higher quality content = better marketing ROI.
3. **Marketplace Network Effect (future)**: If RetroMuscle opens to multiple brands, more brands = more creator opportunities = more creators = more brands want in.

**Category Design:**
- RetroMuscle should define the category: "Managed Creator Content Supply Chain."
- This is NOT an influencer marketplace. It is NOT a talent agency. It is a content factory with human creators at its core.
- Name the category. Frame the problem. Set the evaluation criteria. Become synonymous with managed creator content.

**Building a Category-Defining Company:**
- Start with retro fitness (small market, dominate it).
- Expand to adjacent lifestyle niches (retro fashion, vintage culture).
- Eventually: the platform that any brand uses to build a managed creator network.
- The moat is the creator network + the content pipeline software + the data on what content performs.

**Distribution Strategy:**
- Creators ARE the distribution channel. Every video they produce is marketing for the brand AND proof of concept for other potential creators.
- Developer-equivalent for RetroMuscle: the creator onboarding experience. Make it seamless. Creators who have a great first experience recruit other creators.

**Key Growth Challenges Through Platform Lens:**
1. **Reaching critical mass of creators** - The cold start problem. Seed with hand-recruited creators, then build referral mechanics.
2. **Content quality consistency** - Build a content scoring system. Data network effects make this better over time.
3. **Platform expansion** - When do you open to other brands? Only after the single-brand model is proven and the pipeline is automated.
